What happens if you don't get enough calcium?

  • There may not be any obvious symptoms of inadequate calcium intake over a short-term period because human body takes calcium from the bones and maintains calcium level in the blood.
  • Over a long-term period, calcium intake below the recommended level can lead to low bone mass (osteopenia) and increase the risks of osteoporosis and bone fractures.
  • Symptoms of severe calcium deficiency include numbness and tingling in the fingers, convulsions and abnormal heart rhythms.

Source: NIH. Calcium: Fact sheet for consumers. Available at: http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Calcium-QuickFacts/#h6
